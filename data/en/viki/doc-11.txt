Bertolt Brecht
Eugen Berthold Friedrich Brecht (1898-1956) was a German playwright and poet.
He came to age after the First World War and moved to Berlin, where he wrote The Threepenny Opera.
He had a great influence on modern day theatre.
Brecht was a Marxist and had to fled to Scandinavia and later to the United States.
He returned after the war to East Berlin, which was now communist, and eventually died there.
