Richard II is Shakespeare's only play written entirely in blank verse.
It tells of the forced abdication and murder of Richard II, and his murderer's rise to power as King Henry IV.
It was written about 1595, and probably first performed shortly after the play's completion.
It was first published in quarto form about 1597.
Other quarto editions were published over the years.
In 1623, the play was published in the First Folio.
