Physics
Physics, a branch of science, is the study of all forces and their impacts on the environment.
Modern physics connects ideas together about the four laws of symmetry and conservation (energy, momentum, charge, and parity).
The word physics comes from the Greek word ἡ φύσις "nature".
In addition to these four units, there are three other ones: the mole, which is the unit of the quantity of matter, the candela which measures the luminous intensity (the power of lighting) and the kelvin, the unit of temperature.
