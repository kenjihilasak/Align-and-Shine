Glyptodon
Specimen fossil Glyptodon from National Museum of Natural History, Washington, DC
Glyptodon was one of the biggest ancient armadillos.
Fossils of this mammal have been found in Argentina, South America.
It was about the size and weight of a small car.
Glyptodon lived between 2 million and 10,000 years ago, during the Pleistocene.
These herbivores (plant-eaters) would not have been vulnerable to the Sparassodont carnivores of the day.
The native human population in their range is believed to have hunted them and used the shells for shelter in bad weather.
