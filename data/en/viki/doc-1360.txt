Oratorio
Statue of Handel by Louis-François Roubiliac, 1738
An oratorio is a musical work for orchestra, vocal soloists, and choir.
Oratorios are usually, but not always, based on a Biblical subject.
They are dramatic.
Unlike opera, they are strictly a concert piece and are not staged.
The first oratorio is said to be Emilio de Cavalieri's Rappresentatione di Anima, et di Corpo of 1600.
