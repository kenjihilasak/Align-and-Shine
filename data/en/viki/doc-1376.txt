Vowel
The letters of the English alphabet are either vowels or consonants or both.
A vowel sound comes from the lungs, through the vocal cords, and is not blocked, so there is no friction.
All English words have vowels.
These letters are vowels in English:
A, E, I, O, U, and sometimes Y
The letter Y can be a vowel (as in the word "cry" or "candy"), or it can be a consonant (as in "yellow").
These five or six letters stand for about 20 vowel sounds in most English accents.
This important fact helps to explain why pronunciation can be difficult for both native speakers and learners of English.
