Opera
Performance of Lully's Armide at the Palais-Royal in 1761.
An opera is a work for the stage that unites music and drama.
An opera is completely sung from beginning to end.
Operas are written for orchestra, vocal soloists, and chorus.
Opera had its origin in RenaissanceItaly.
It was an attempt to recreate the dramas of ancient Greece and Rome in the manner in which they were believed to have been first performed.
All operas consist of recitatives and arias, duets, trios, and other vocal combinations.
Originally, operas were entertainments for the court.
Statue of Handel by Louis-François Roubiliac, 1738
Opera was opened to the general public in Venice, Italy, during the 1637 Carnival season.
In England, Henry Purcell wrote the first English opera, Dido and Aeneas.
It was first performed in 1688.
In France, Jean-Baptiste Lully wrote many operas.
Operas of the Baroque period were based on Greek and Roman mythology.
Gluck thought opera had strayed from its purpose.
He reformed opera, with his focus on drama and vocal expression of that drama.
His greatest operas include Orpheus and Euridice and Alceste.
