Outlaw
An outlaw or bandit is a person who is "outside the law" usually because they have committed serious crimes.
A longtime criminal can be declared an outlaw, which means the outlaw cannot use the legal system to protect himself if needed.
Robin Hood, the English folk hero, who was probably a combination of several real and legendary outlaws.
Carmine Crocco, the most famous Italian outlaw of the XIX century, an escaped convict who joined the liberal revolution of Garibaldi but returned to banditry because he didn't get the promised forgiveness.
Then he became the leader of 2.000 men, heading the most feared peasant army in southern Italy at the time.
Ned Kelly, the Australian bushranger.
The government passed a law on 30 October 1878 to make the Kelly gang outlaws.
They no longer had any legal rights.
They could be killed by anyone.
