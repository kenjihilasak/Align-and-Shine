Canary Islands
Even as Mellila and Ceuta, the Canary Islands are one of the last remains of what once the Spanish Empire was.
The Canaries formed the main stopover for Spanish ships, who were going to or came back from America.
The islands are a bridge between Europa, Africa and America.
Since 1982, the Canary Islands are an autonomous region.
They're a part of the European Union, like the Spanish mainland.
