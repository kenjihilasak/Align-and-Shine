Madeira
In 1419, the archipelago was discovered by some Portuguese sailors and a year later the Portuguese settled on the islands.
Today, Madeira is a popular tourist destination.
It is noted for its nature, good climate, historical and cultural value and above all the Madeira wine.
In the 80s, the islands were a special economic zone to boost the local economy.
Some businesses still have offices in Madeira, for they have to less taxes over there.
The islands are, like Portugal, a part of the European Union.
