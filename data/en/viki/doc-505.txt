Nauru
In the late 1960s and early 1970s, the island government allowed too much mining of the valuable phosphate mineral, so now the reserves on Nauru are almost entirely depleted.
Phosphate mining has left a large zagged crater in the middle of the island, with only a narrow strip of land that surrounds the island perimeter.
Mining has stripped and ruined about 80 percent of Nauru's land area.
About half of the native fish and marine life have been killed by the silt runoff of phosphate rock.
This has greatly harmed the economy of the country.
