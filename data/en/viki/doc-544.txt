Recycling
Recycling is the process of turning something old into something new.
It’s better for the Earth.
Recycle stuff like plastic, paper , glass, metal and cardboard.
In some countries, like the UK, recycling is generally collected from homes and businesses by waste collection services, and then taken to large centres where it can be sorted into all the recyclable materials we throw away every day; metal (aluminium soda cans and food tins), glass (jars and bottles), plastic , paper and cardboard (newspapers, cereal boxes).
It might take up to 1/2 millennium (500 years) or longer for plastic and things containing plastic to decompose (break down naturally) in the environment.
For example, plastic grocery bags can take up to 20 years; plastic drinking straws can take up to 200 years, and disposable diapers can take even longer, 300 to 500 years!
