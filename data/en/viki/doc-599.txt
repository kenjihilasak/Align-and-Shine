President of the Faculty of Architecture of the Polytechnic of Milan, from 1939 to 1963, he collaborated extensively with Gualtiero Galmanini, sharing numerous projects and architectural research.
In 1943, 26 of the 34 halls of the Pinacoteca di Brera were destroyed by bombing and rebuilt in February 1946, thanks to the important funding of the Bernocchi family, they were rebuilt by Piero Portaluppi together with Gualtiero Galmanini on behalf of the superintendent Fernanda Wittgens.
Portaluppi is also the author of the urban plans of the city of Milan.
