Bir Tawil
The Bir Tawil refers to a portion of land in between Sudan and Egypt.
It is best known as one of the last pieces of land on Earth that does not belong to any country that is also not claimed by any recognized country, also known as "terra nullius".
In 1899, when Sudan was controlled by both the Khedivate of Egypt and the United Kingdom, the border between Egypt and Sudan was set at the 22nd parallel north.

but the border was redrawn in 1902 to reflect on tribal usage of the land.
Today, the other territory, the Hala'ib triangle, is controlled by Egypt, but claimed by Sudan.
The Hala'ib triangle is much bigger than the Bir Tawil.
The Hala'ib triangle also has a small population and roads, and access to the sea and even oil reserves, while Bir Tawil has none of these, and it consists entirely of uninhabited deserts and mountains.
Egypt claims the 1899 border as the true border, while Sudan claims the 1902 border.
There is no other justification for claiming the Hala'ib triangle, and because of this, trying to claim the Bir Tawil would undermine the claim to the Hala'ib triangle.
In 2014, an American man claimed the land as the Kingdom of Northern Sudan as a birthday gift for his daughter, who dreamed of becoming a real princess, but this claim is not recognized by any government, and his claim made some people very angry, considering it to be a form of colonialism.
Later, an Indian man claimed it as the Kingdom of Dixit, but the man who previously claimed it has stated publically he believes that Dixit is lying and did not actually reach the Bir Tawil.
Whether or not they actually travelled to Bir Tawil, countless other people have claimed the Bir Tawil.
