Zeus
Zeus was married to Hera, the Queen of Heaven and the goddess of marriage.
He had several children by Hera including Hebe, Ares, and Hephaestus.
He had other children by other women, which infuriated Hera.
The god Apollo and the goddess Artemis, for example, were his children by Leto, the goddess of Motherhood.
Dionysus, the god of wine and the theater, was Zeus's son by the mortal woman, Semele.
The hero Herakles was the son of Zeus and Alkmene, a mortal woman.
There are many others.
Zeus had two brothers who shared the rule of men and the natural world.
Hades was the Lord of the Underworld, and Poseidon was the Ruler of the Oceans.
Both were infrequent visitors to Mt Olympus.
