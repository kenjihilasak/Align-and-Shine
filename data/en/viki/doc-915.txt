Rhine
A map of the River Rhine
The Rhine River (German: Rhein, French: Rhin, Dutch: Rijn) is 1,230 km (764 mi) long.
883 km (549 mi) can be used by ships.
It is one of the longest rivers in Europe.
Its name comes from the Celtic word "renos".
Renos means raging flow.
The river runs only into one lake on its way, this is Lake Constance that is on the border of Switzerland, Germany and Austria.
Along its banks are several notorious castles in which several men, known as robber barons lived.
It was a quiet river, until the Industrial Revolution hit Europe, and factories set up along its banks and emptied all their waste into the River.
The Salmon left and every living thing died.
Then after World War II, the Rhine Action Programme was set up to boost the Rhine's wildlife and reduce the pollution there.
