Angola
All eighteen provincies numbered
Angola was, until November 1, a Portuguese colony.
That day the people of Angola celebrate Independence day.
From 1975 to 2002 there was a civil war and since 2002 Angola has been a republic.
Angola consists of the jungle, the desert and the mountains, with the Morro Moco as the highest point (2,620 meters).
Angola is also rich in mineral resources: petroleum, iron ore, copper ore, bauxite, phosphate, uranium ore and diamonds.
The capital of Angola is Luanda, the population is near 25 million people, and the area is 1,246,700 km².
The official language of Angola is Portuguese, because it was a colony of Portugal.
